Transports

About Public Transportation

Amsterdam's public transport system is efficient and well-connected, with trains, metros, trams, buses, and ferries covering the city. The OV-Chipkaart is a convenient travel card that can be used across all forms of transport. It can be bought in stores, at OV-Chipkaart machines, shops, or with a bank card. Visit OV-Chipkaart for detailed information.

Download the 9292 app for detailed schedules and routes of trams and buses. Simply type your starting location and destination, and the app displays the tram and bus numbers and routes.

Download the NS app for train and metro schedules, route and platform numbers similar to the 9292 app. Type the starting station and destination station, and the app displays the details of the train going to that station.

The Available Public Transport in Amsterdam

  • Trams: Amsterdam's extensive tram network covers most parts of the city. Trams are a convenient way to get around the city center and reach popular attractions.
  • Buses: Amsterdam's bus network complements the tram system and extends to neighborhoods not covered by trams. Buses are operated by GVB (Gemeentelijk Vervoerbedrijf), the city's public transport company.
  • Metro: The Amsterdam Metro system has four lines (M1, M2, M3, and M4) that connect the city center with suburbs and areas like Amsterdam Arena and RAI Convention Centre. It's a fast way to travel longer distances within the city.
  • Trains: Amsterdam has several train stations, with Amsterdam Centraal being the main hub. Trains are a great way to explore other Dutch cities and regions, including day trips to places like Haarlem, Utrecht, and Rotterdam.
  • Ferries: Amsterdam is known for its canals, and several ferries operate across the IJ River, connecting the city center to the north. These ferries provide access to attractions like Eye Film Museum and NDSM Wharf.
